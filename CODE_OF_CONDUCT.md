# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in
this project and its community a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people;
- respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences;
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback;
- accepting responsibility, apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience;
- focusing on what is best for the community and project, not only what is best
  for us as individuals;
- reviewing technical claims, benchmark evidence, and code rigorously without
  attacking the person who submitted them.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information, such as a physical or email
  address, without explicit permission;
- deliberately exposing credentials, prompts, voice samples, personal data, or
  private security reports;
- intimidation, retaliation, or pressure directed at a reporter, reviewer, or
  contributor;
- other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these
standards. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to
behavior they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, documentation, issues, and other contributions that do
not align with this Code of Conduct. When appropriate, they will communicate
the reason for a moderation decision.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces. It also applies when an
individual officially represents the project in public spaces. Examples of
representing the project include using an official project account, posting
through an official communication channel, or acting as an appointed
representative at an online or offline event.

Project spaces include source control, issues, pull requests, reviews,
benchmark discussions, release work, private project correspondence, and other
official collaboration channels.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported privately to the project maintainer at
[luka@lukaloehr.com](mailto:luka@lukaloehr.com) with the subject
`Qwen3-TTS Native conduct report`.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and fairly. The maintainer will respect
the privacy and security of the reporter as far as reasonably possible and
will avoid conflicts of interest. If a report concerns the person receiving
it, the reporter may ask a trusted GitHub platform representative or relevant
hosting provider to handle platform-level abuse.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will use these community-impact guidelines when determining the
consequences for an action:

### 1. Correction

**Community impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior considered
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning that explains the violation and why
the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community impact:** A violation through a single incident or a series of
actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. The person
must not interact with those involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing this Code of Conduct, for a specified period. This may include
avoiding interactions in community spaces and external channels. Violating
these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary ban

**Community impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication
with the community for a specified period. During this period, the person must
not interact publicly or privately with those involved, including those
enforcing this Code of Conduct. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent
ban.

### 4. Permanent ban

**Community impact:** A pattern of violating community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression
toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from all public interaction within the
community.
